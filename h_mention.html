Atari


Atari is a brand name that has been owned by several entities since its inception in 1972. It is currently owned by French company Atari SA (formerly Infogrames) through a subsidiary named Atari Interactive. The original Atari, Inc., founded in Sunnyvale, California, USA in 1972 by Nolan Bushnell and Ted Dabney, was a pioneer in arcade games, home video game consoles, and home computers. The company's products, such as Pong and the Atari 2600, helped define the electronic entertainment industry from the 1970s to the mid-1980s.

Sega



Sega's a name synonymous with gaming, but their console story is a tale of arcade origins and home console battles. Founded in the 1940s, they rose to prominence in the arcade scene during the 60s and 80s with classics like OutRun. In the console wars, Sega entered the ring in the 80s with the SG-1000, but truly made a splash with the lightning-fast Sega Genesis and its mascot, Sonic the Hedgehog. The Saturn and Dreamcast brought innovation, but stiff competition ultimately led Sega to abandon console development in 2001. Despite this, Sega continues to thrive as a third-party game developer, bringing beloved franchises like Total War to life on other platforms. So, while they may not be building consoles anymore, Sega's legacy of arcade hits, iconic characters, and enduring games remains a permanent fixture in the gaming world.
