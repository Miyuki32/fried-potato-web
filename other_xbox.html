The Xbox (2001)

Xbox has come a long way since its debut in 2001, evolving into a global gaming powerhouse with cutting-edge technology and a vibrant community.With Xbox Game Pass, backward compatibility, and exclusive game studios, Xbox offers a diverse and inclusive gaming experience for players worldwide.

Xbox 360 Pro (2005)

The Xbox 360 Pro, also known as the Xbox 360 Premium, is a version of Microsoft's Xbox 360 gaming console that was positioned between the Xbox 360 Arcade and the Xbox 360 Elite models.The Xbox 360 Pro was well-regarded for its balance of features and price, making it a popular choice for gamers looking for a capable and versatile gaming console without the higher price tag of the Elite model.

Xbox 360 core (2005)

The Xbox 360 Core is a base-level model in Microsoft's Xbox 360 console lineup, introduced in 2005. It is designed to provide an entry-level gaming experience at a more affordable price point.

Xbox 360 Elite (2007)

The Xbox 360 Elite is a premium version of Microsoft's Xbox 360, released in 2007. It features a sleek matte black finish, a 120GB hard drive for ample storage, and an HDMI port for 1080p HD gaming. The console includes a black wireless controller and a wired headset. It supports a vast game library, Xbox Live for online gaming and digital content, and some backwards compatibility with original Xbox games. The Xbox 360 Elite is known for its enhanced storage, superior HD capabilities, and stylish design.

Xbox 360 Arcade (2007)

The Xbox 360 Arcade, released in 2007, is an entry-level version of the Xbox 360. It features a sleek white design, a 512 MB internal flash memory, and a bundled selection of classic Xbox Live Arcade games. It includes a wireless controller and various connectivity options like USB ports and an Ethernet port. The console supports streaming services and can play DVDs and CDs. The Xbox 360 Arcade is a budget-friendly option that can be upgraded with additional storage and peripherals.

Xbox 360 S (2010)

The Xbox 360 S, or Xbox 360 Slim, launched in June 2010, features a sleek design, built-in 802.11n Wi-Fi, and options for 4GB or 250GB storage. It has improved cooling, quieter operation, and additional USB ports. Supporting HDMI and Kinect, it offers enhanced performance and reliability, making it a modern, robust gaming console.

Xbox 360 E (2013)

The Xbox 360 E is Microsoft's third version of the Xbox 360, featuring a sleek design, high-definition gaming, built-in Wi-Fi, and support for physical and digital content.

Xbox One (2013)

The Xbox One is the third video game console made by Microsoft, succeeding the Xbox 360. It was announced on May 21, 2013. It was first released on November 22, 2013, ahead of Sony's PlayStation 4. Microsoft marketed the Xbox One as an "all-in-one entertainment system", which is where its name comes from.

Xbox One Elite(2015)

Aiming to be the Rolls Royce of Xbox Ones in a sea of Ford and Chevy bundles and deals, the Xbox One Elite Console offers a premium alternative at a premium $499 price: a 1TB Xbox One – complete with a solid state hybrid drive (SSHD) – paired with Microsoft’s upscale Elite controller.
